COLUMBIA RIVER GORGE/MT HOOD TOUR - Bus tour and visit to nationally known Multnomah Falls, Bonneville Dam (Fish Hatchery, Trout Pond, Sturgeon Center, and Visitor's Center), and historic Timberline Lodge on Mt. Hood.
Tour fee: $50.00.

Multomah Falls / Columbia River Gorge

A waterfall as magnificent and memorable as any in the country is located just a 30- minute drive outside of Portland. Visiting Multnomah Falls, a 611-foot-tall roaring, awe-inspiring cascade of icy water, lets you experience the power and beauty of nature up close and with ease.


Bonneville Dam / Fish Hatchery / Sturgeon Center

Bonneville Lock and Dam is located about 40 miles east of Portland, Ore. Built in 1938 to improve navigation on the Columbia River and to provide hydropower to the Pacific Northwest. The Nearby Bonneville fish hatchery display ponds offer a relaxing place to feed large rainbow trout and view adult white sturgeon measuring more than 10 feet long.


Mt Hood / Timberline Lodge

Mount Hood, called Wy'east by the Multnomah tribe, is Oregon's highest mountain. Timberline Lodge National Historic Landmark, built in the late 1930s during the great depression, sits at an elevation of 5,960 feet. Workers used large timbers and local stone, and placed intricately carved decorative elements throughout the building.
